Highlights of Fred's Life

  • 1899 - Born as Frederick Austerlitz in Omaha, Nebraska
  • 1905 - Moved to New York and began training at the Alviene Master School of the Theatre and Academy of Cultural Arts
  • 1917 - Performed on Broadway for the first time in Over the Top with Adele
  • 1932 - Ended his partnership with his sister Adele when she married
  • 1933 - Debuted in Hollywood in Dancing Lady with Joan Crawford. Also first performed with Ginger Rogers in Flying Down to Rio, beginning his most significant dance partnership as well as a period of starring in many films
  • 1946 - Retired from film
  • 1947 - Founded Fred Astaire Dance Studios
  • 1948 - Returned to film in Easter Parade
  • 1952 - Recorded The Astaire Story, an musical overview of Astaire's career to that point, which would in 1999 receive the Grammy Hall of Fame Award
  • 1958 - Starred in An Evening with Fred Astaire, which won nine Emmy's including 'Best Single Performance by an Actor'
  • 1969 - Starred in his last major film, Finian's Rainbow
  • 1974 - Danced with Jennifer Jones in The Towering Inferno and receiving an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor
  • 1979 - Made a guest appearance in Battlestar Galactica due to his grandchildren's interest in the series, and was the last time he danced on screen
  • 1981 - Performed in his final film Ghost Story
  • 1987 - Died at age 88 of pneumonia
